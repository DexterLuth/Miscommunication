Based on the applicable regulations and the client's intent, the financial institution must disclose the following requirements to the client:

### Required Disclosures to the Client

**1. Truth in Lending Act (TILA) / Regulation Z Disclosures (for each credit product considered: personal loan & line of credit):**

*   **Annual Percentage Rate (APR):** The true cost of credit expressed as an annual rate, considering all finance charges.
*   **Finance Charge:** The total dollar amount the credit will cost over the life of the loan.
*   **Amount Financed:** The amount of credit provided to the consumer.
*   **Total of Payments:** The total amount the consumer will have paid after making all scheduled payments.
*   **Payment Schedule:** The number, amount, and timing of payments.
*   **Interest Rate Type:** Clearly state whether the rate is fixed or variable.
    *   **For Variable Rates (e.g., typically Lines of Credit):** Explain how the rate is determined (e.g., index plus margin), conditions under which it may change, and any limitations on changes.
*   **Late Payment Fees:** Clearly disclose any charges for late payments.
*   **Prepayment Penalties:** Disclose if there are any penalties for paying off the loan early (if applicable).
*   **Security Interest:** If the loan is secured by any collateral, this must be disclosed.

**2. Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) / Regulation V Disclosures:**

*   **Adverse Action Notice:** If the customer is denied credit or offered less favorable terms based on information in their credit report, the bank must provide a written notice stating:
    *   The specific reasons for the adverse action.
    *   The name, address, and phone number of the consumer reporting agency that provided the report.
    *   A statement that the consumer reporting agency did not make the decision to deny credit and cannot provide specific reasons.
    *   The consumer's right to obtain a free copy of their credit report from the agency within 60 days.
    *   The consumer's right to dispute the accuracy or completeness of any information in their credit report.
*   **Credit Score Disclosure:** If a credit score was used in making the credit decision, the bank must provide:
    *   The score itself.
    *   The range of possible scores.
    *   The key factors that adversely affected the score.
    *   The date the score was created.
    *   The name of the consumer reporting agency that provided the score.
*   **Permissible Purpose for Credit Inquiry:** Inform the client that a credit check will be performed and explain the permissible purpose (e.g., for evaluating a credit application).

**3. Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) / Regulation B Disclosures:**

*   **Adverse Action Notice:** Similar to FCRA, if credit is denied or unfavorable terms are offered, the bank must provide a notice explaining the specific reasons or advising the applicant of their right to request specific reasons within a certain timeframe. This notice must also include the ECOA statement about non-discrimination.
*   **Non-Discrimination Assurance:** While not a specific form, the bank's practices and disclosures must implicitly assure the client that the application will be evaluated fairly and without discrimination based on protected characteristics.

**4. Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP) - Dodd-Frank Act / CFPB Implications:**

*   **Clear, Conspicuous, and Accurate Information:** All information provided, including marketing, product descriptions, and advice, must be presented in a way that is easy to understand, prominent, and free from misleading statements or omissions.
*   **Transparent Product Comparisons:** When comparing personal loans and lines of credit, the bank must clearly explain the pros and cons of each *relative to the client's stated preferences* (e.g., highlighting the fixed nature and predictable payments of a personal loan versus the variable interest rate and flexible drawdowns of a line of credit).
*   **Material Terms and Conditions:** All material terms, conditions, and potential risks (e.g., variable rates increasing, fees) must be disclosed clearly and comprehensively.

**5. Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) / Anti-Money Laundering (AML) - Customer Identification Program (CIP) Disclosures:**

*   **Identity Verification Notice:** The bank must provide a notice to the customer stating that it is required by federal law to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account.
